## TIER 2 — DECADE-BY-DECADE POLICY HISTORY

### 1950s — Foundation years

**Context:** Singapore gains self-governance (1959). Colonial-era poverty, overcrowding in kampungs, no public housing system, no social safety net.

**Key events:**

- 1955: CPF established (contribution rate 5% employer + 5% employee)
- 1957: Education Act passed; free primary education introduced
- 1959: HDB's predecessor (SIT — Singapore Improvement Trust) replaced by HDB

**Ingest:**

- https://www.cpf.gov.sg/member/infohub/educational-resources/from-paper-to-pixels-how-your-cpf-statements-have-changed-over-the-last-70-years
- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singapore_Improvement_Trust
- https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Provident_Fund#History

---

### 1960s — Nation-building decade

**Context:** Independence (1965). Mass resettlement from kampungs to HDB flats. Land acquisition at scale. Rapid industrialisation.

**Key policy milestones:**

- 1960: HDB established; emergency housing drive begins (target 110,000 units 1960–1970)
- 1964: HDB Home Ownership for the People Scheme — flats sold on 99-year leasehold
- 1966: Land Acquisition Act — government can acquire private land at low cost
- 1968: CPF savings allowed to be used for HDB flat purchase (game-changing)
- 1968: Employment Act passed — basic employment rights for workers
- 1969: Stop At Two family planning policy introduced

### 1970s — Economic take-off

**Context:** Rapid GDP growth. Industrialisation. First generation of HDB flat owners. Workforce formalisation.

**Key policy milestones:**

- 1972: HDB upgrading programmes begin; larger flat types introduced
- 1973: NTUC FairPrice co-operative established (price stabilisation)
- 1975: Workmen's Compensation Act — injury protection for workers
- 1977: CPF contribution rates raised significantly (employers and employees)
- 1979: "Wages correction" policy — deliberate wage increases to move up value chain

### 1980s — Maturation and refinement

**Context:** Singapore becomes a middle-income country. Housing largely solved. Shift from survival to quality of life. First major policy adjustments.

**Key policy milestones:**

- 1981: HDB resale market officially opened — flat owners can sell on open market
- 1984: MediSave introduced — CPF sub-account for hospitalisation (first in Asia)
- 1984: CPF contribution rate peaks at 50% (25% employer + 25% employee)
- 1985: Recession — CPF employer rate cut from 25% to 10% to restore competitiveness
- 1986: HDB upgrading — Main Upgrading Programme (MUP) begins
- 1987: MRT opens — public transport transformed
- 1988: Town Councils Act — MPs made responsible for managing HDB estates
- 1989: Ethnic Integration Policy (EIP) — racial quota for HDB blocks

### 1990s — Restructuring for the future

**Context:** Economic maturity. Ageing population concerns begin. MediShield introduced. CPF Investment Scheme. HDB resale prices surge.

**Key policy milestones:**

- 1990: MediShield introduced — catastrophic illness insurance funded by CPF
- 1991: CPF Minimum Sum Scheme — ensuring retirement adequacy
- 1992: ElderShield introduced (long-term care for severe disability)
- 1993: HDB resale levy introduced — discourages multiple subsidised flat purchases
- 1994: COE (Certificate of Entitlement) system introduced for vehicles
- 1995: Edusave introduced — savings account for every Singapore student
- 1997: Asian Financial Crisis — HDB Special CPF Housing Grant introduced for lower-income buyers
- 1998: Workfare precursor schemes begin for lower-wage workers
- 1999: Medifund established — safety net for those who cannot afford hospital bills

### 2000s — Knowledge economy and social safety net

**Context:** Dot-com bust, SARS (2003), Global Financial Crisis (2008). Workforce restructuring. First serious debate about income inequality. Workfare and social assistance formalised.

**Key policy milestones:**

- 2001: Economic Restructuring Shares and New Singapore Shares — direct cash to citizens during downturn
- 2003: SARS crisis — MOH emergency response frameworks established
- 2006: Workfare Income Supplement (WIS) introduced — wage supplement for lower-income workers; first formal anti-poverty transfer programme
- 2007: GST raised from 5% to 7%; GST Offset Package introduced
- 2007: ElderShield enhanced — higher payouts, more conditions covered
- 2008: Global Financial Crisis — Resilience Package, Jobs Credit Scheme
- 2009: CHAS (Community Health Assist Scheme) introduced — subsidised GP visits for lower and middle-income Singaporeans
- 2009: HDB income ceiling raised to broaden access

### 2010s — Pioneer Generation, CPF Life, and housing cooling measures

**Context:** Ageing population a central policy concern. Housing prices soar (2010–2013). Government intervenes with cooling measures. CPF Life replaces CPF Minimum Sum. Pioneer Generation Package.

**Key policy milestones:**

- 2010–2013: Property market cooling measures — Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty (ABSD), Total Debt Servicing Ratio (TDSR), Mortgage Servicing Ratio (MSR)
- 2012: HDB income ceiling raised from $8,000 to $10,000 for new flats
- 2013: CPF LIFE (Lifelong Income For the Elderly) made default — monthly payouts for life replacing lump-sum withdrawal at 55
- 2013: Population White Paper — sparked national debate on immigration and population policy
- 2014: Pioneer Generation Package — $8 billion package for Singaporeans born before 1950; Medisave top-ups, CHAS subsidies, Pioneer Generation Disability Assistance Scheme
- 2014: MediShield Life review — mandatory for all; higher coverage; no lifetime limits; pre-existing conditions covered
- 2015: MediShield Life fully launched — replaces MediShield
- 2015: Silver Support Scheme announced — quarterly cash for elderly poor
- 2015: SkillsFuture Credit ($500 for all adults 25+) — lifelong learning
- 2016: HDB announces VERS (Voluntary Early Redevelopment Scheme) concept
- 2017: Merdeka Generation Package for those born 1950–1959
- 2018: MOH announces CareShield Life to replace ElderShield from 2020
- 2019: Bicentennial Bonus — one-off payments to mark 200 years
- 2019: Significant HDB grant restructuring — EHG replaces AHG and SHG, removing income ceiling for grant eligibility

### 2020s — COVID, cost of living, and social resilience

**Context:** COVID-19 pandemic reshapes everything. Unprecedented fiscal support. Recovery packages. Cost of living rises sharply post-pandemic. Budget 2023 raises GST to 9%. Significant social policy expansion.

**Key policy milestones:**

- 2020: COVID-19 — $100 billion in support packages; Jobs Support Scheme (JSS); Solidarity Payment ($600 to all adults); Circuit Breaker
- 2020: Enhanced housing grants restructured; BTO delivery timeline extended
- 2021: SingapoRediscovers vouchers; SME support extended
- 2022: Cost of living rises; CDC Vouchers introduced ($100 per household)
- 2023: GST raised to 9%; $3 billion Assurance Package (AP) to cushion impact — cash payouts, MediSave top-ups, U-Save, S&CC rebates
- 2023: HDB launches PLH (Prime Location Public Housing) model — tighter resale restrictions for flats in prime areas
- 2023: HDB BTO classification revamped — Standard, Plus, Prime replacing mature/non-mature estate categories
- 2024: CPF contribution rates for older workers raised
- 2024: Significant MediShield Life enhancements — higher claim limits
- 2025: Budget 2025 — SG60 bonus for all citizens, further CPF rate increases, MediSave withdrawal limit increases
- 2025: CPF OW ceiling raised to $7,400 (further raised to $8,000 in 2026)
- 2026: Fresh Start Housing Scheme enhanced; CareShield Life premiums revised
